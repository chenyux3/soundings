# Your recurring patterns

This file is what makes Soundings *yours*. It holds the traps, boundaries, and values-forks you keep hitting — so the council can recognize them and get sharper each session instead of starting cold every time. It's the mechanism behind the promise of **clarity that compounds**: each session can deposit a lens you carry into the next.

It ships **empty on purpose.** Fill it after your first few sessions, once real patterns have surfaced — not from imagination on day one.

---

## How it's used — and the one guardrail

At the start of each session, the council reads this file. If something here fits your question, an advisor will surface it **as an assumption for you to confirm — never as a conclusion it reasons forward from.**

That guardrail is load-bearing. The point is to catch a pattern *early*, not to fit you to last month's trap and miss what's genuinely new in today's decision. This is the same assumptions-first discipline every advisor follows (`SKILL.md` → Operating principles). A stored pattern is a hypothesis the council tests against you, not a verdict it applies to you.

---

## What to put here

Three kinds of entry. Keep each to a line or two — a pattern, not an essay.

### Recurring traps
Thinking patterns that have cost you before — the shape of how you get stuck.
> *Example:* Looks at the same question from many angles until talked out of acting.
> *Example:* When one imperfect result lands, reacts by wanting to rebuild the whole thing from the bottom.

### Boundaries to protect
Lines you've decided you don't want to cross, and what crossing them costs you.
> *Example:* Roles that require live negotiation drain disproportionately — design around posted terms, not haggling.
> *Example:* Family-first is a hard constraint, not a preference to be optimized against.

### Values-forks you keep returning to
The same underlying tradeoff showing up across different decisions.
> *Example:* Autonomy vs. reach — keeps choosing autonomy; flag when a path quietly trades it away.

---

## Where entries come from

The best entries are ones a session *named* for you — the structural insight from a Phase D synthesis, or the pattern a Phase E next step turned on. When a session surfaces a durable pattern (not a one-off feeling), add it here in your own words. Over time this file becomes the council's memory of how you think — which is exactly what a real advisor who has known you for years would have, and a cold prompt never will.
