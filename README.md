# Soundings

**A council for the personal decisions that resist a clean answer — it reads the depth you can't see from the surface, and leaves you with clarity that compounds.**

Before you navigate a hard decision, you take a sounding. Soundings convenes a small council of independent advisors that read the depth under your question: the assumption you're standing on, the values-fork underneath, the pattern you keep repeating. You come back up with a **clearer question** — and a lens you carry into the next decision.

It's a [Claude Code](https://claude.com/claude-code) skill. You type `/soundings <your question>`, and a structured consultation runs in the conversation.

---

## What it's for

The decisions that don't have a right answer you can look up — direction, role, founder/co-founder fit, a family-first call, a life-stage fork. The kind where more information doesn't help because the hard part isn't *facts*, it's *which value you're actually optimizing for*.

Soundings doesn't tell you what to do. Its job is to **structure your thinking** so you can see the decision clearly and recognize what aligns with your values. You stay the decider.

---

## What "worked" means

A session succeeded if, at the end, you can say **yes** to both:

1. **Is the question clearer than when I started?** (Not more complex — *clearer*.)
2. **Did at least one advisor surface a perspective I didn't already hold, that I now believe is accurate?**

That second test is the whole point. If the council only ever reflects what you already think, it's a mirror, not a council.

---

## How it works

Five phases, run in conversation:

- **A — Topic.** You state the question, what's hard about it, and what you've already considered.
- **B — Selection.** The chair recommends 2–3 advisors from your roster, one line of reasoning each. You confirm or override.
- **C — Independent skeletons.** Each advisor drafts *without seeing the others*. Each leads with the **1–2 assumptions** its view rests on, gives a short structural skeleton, and asks you **one** question. No advice yet.
- **D — Synthesis.** The chair surfaces where the advisors converge, where they conflict, and the **values-fork** the decision actually sits on — then closes with a *conditional* ("if value X, path A; if value Y, path B"), never a recommendation. The chair adds no opinion of its own.
- **E — Your next step.** You commit to exactly one: pick a direction, gather one fact, revisit later, reformulate the question, or **go deeper** into a round 2 with the advisors you choose.

A depth loop (round 2 and beyond) is available whenever you want it, and the session ends when *you* close it — not when the model decides it's done.

---

## How this differs from a "board of advisors" prompt

There are a lot of "personal board of advisors" tools. Three things make this one different:

1. **Method-of-inquiry advisors, not personas.** Advisors aren't simulated founders or famous thinkers. Each is a *lens* — a characteristic question it asks — and the framework it uses is pulled fresh per session. No impersonation, no name-dropping, no canned playbook.

2. **A discipline against telling you what you want to hear.** Advisors draft *independently* (no echo), they **lead with their assumptions and wait for you to confirm them** before reasoning forward (no confident-wrong-template), and the chair is forbidden from adding a fourth opinion. The structure is built to *disagree with itself* and to catch its own over-fitting — the opposite of a model that agrees with your framing and elaborates.

3. **An emotional layer most boards don't have.** A panel of operator voices (CFO, CMO, a famous founder) reasons about the *external* problem. Soundings includes a **Therapist** advisor whose entire method is the emotional content *underneath* the surface question — the part that's protecting something, the driver that persists even after you intellectually agree. This is why Soundings sessions so often **dissolve** the question instead of answering it: the surface strategic question turns out to be a proxy for an emotional one, and only that lens catches it. The example below is exactly this.

And because it remembers (see [Make it yours](#make-it-yours)), the clarity **compounds** — each hard decision starts sharper than the last, instead of starting cold.

## A session, start to finish

A real session, lightly de-identified. Watch the question *dissolve* rather than get answered — and watch the fix turn out to run **opposite** to the obvious advice.

**The question (Phase A).**
> `/soundings When I collaborate, I instinctively set my idea apart from what others are doing — I emphasize the difference. Someone I trust pointed out that people mostly adopt what already resonates with them, so it's better to frame an initiative as a natural extension of what they're already doing. I agree intellectually, but my habit still pulls toward the difference. What's the right balance — different enough not to bury what's new, not so different that adoption stalls?`

**Selection (Phase B).** Three advisors, one line each:
- *Founder coach* — the literal adoption-cost-vs-novelty lever.
- *Therapist* — the habit persists *after* you intellectually agree; that gap is emotional, not tactical.
- *Career coach* — emphasizing difference may be a trained skill misfiring in the wrong room.

**Independent skeletons (Phase C).** Each leads with assumptions, ends with one question.
- **Founder coach** (minimum viable difference) — *Assumption: collaborators have an existing frame to anchor to.* Substance is fixed; framing-distance is a free dial — lead with continuity, land *one* sharp delta. **Q:** when an idea of yours actually got adopted, did you lead with the difference or the continuity?
- **Therapist** (parts work) — *Assumption: under the habit sits a need for your authorship to be recognized.* The difference-urge does double duty — epistemic precision (retrainable) and emotional recognition (won't retrain until the need is met elsewhere). **Q:** picture the idea framed as *their* extension, them running with it — relief, or quiet loss?
- **Career coach** (environment-fit) — *Assumption: you move between peer-review contexts and buy-in contexts.* Emphasizing difference is *competence* in the first, a liability in the second; the skill is noticing which room you're in. **Q:** where has emphasizing difference *served* you recently?

**Your corrections — where it turns.**
> *To Founder coach:* "I already lead with the goal and what should be done. I think I express *too little* about where the idea came from, not too much."
> *To Therapist:* "It's not about recognition. Framing it as their extension feels like I'm not honoring my own honesty — twisting what's true for better reception."

Two assumptions inverted at once: you don't *over*-emphasize difference, you *under*-state provenance — and the driver isn't credit, it's honesty.

**Depth round (Phase C′).**
- **Founder coach** un-bundles what every pitch carries: **provenance** (where it came from — true, rarely needed live) and **value** (what it does for their goal — drives adoption). The error was never over-differentiating; it's leaving provenance *unrecorded*. Protocol: their goal → what it does for it → the one difference that changes what they should *do* → provenance on the written record.
- **Therapist** tests whether honesty is the whole story, or also a fear of the idea being absorbed into theirs: *if your authorship were guaranteed on the record, would framing around their goal still feel like a betrayal?*
  > **You:** No — that feels fine.

  Not a separateness fear, then. With provenance safe on the record, framing around what others care about is comfortable. The whole thing was about the *record*, not the framing.

**Synthesis (Phase D).** The chair adds no opinion — it names what moved:
> The opening question assumed a tradeoff to *balance* — novelty vs. adoption. There is no tradeoff. You already frame for resonance; the deficit is the opposite of the one you named — too *little* provenance recorded, not too much difference. The fix is **additive** (record where your thinking came from), not **subtractive** (differentiate less). The urge to emphasize difference was a recorded-provenance need leaking out in the wrong place.

**Your next step (Phase E).**
> A standing habit: one or two lines of provenance on each initiative — what principle or data it came from, what's genuinely new — on the record. Then stop policing the difference in live conversation.

Notice what it never did: it never gave you a "balance." It **dissolved** the question — the thing you came to *manage* turned out not to exist, and the real fix ran in the *opposite* direction from the obvious advice. That's what a council that tests its own assumptions buys you.

## Make it yours

Two files turn a generic council into *your* council — and turn one-time clarity into clarity that compounds:

- **`roster.md`** — who sits on the council. The seed roster is one person's setup. Replace the advisors with lenses that fit *your* decisions. See **`ROSTER_GUIDE.md`** for how to add, replace, and prune advisors (and the de-duplication test that keeps the council sharp).
- **`self_context.md`** — your recurring traps, boundaries, and the values-forks you keep returning to. It ships **empty**; fill it after your first few sessions. The council reads it each time and surfaces a matching pattern as an *assumption to confirm* — never a verdict. This is the memory a years-long advisor would have and a cold prompt never does.

## Install

Soundings is a Claude Code skill — a folder the CLI loads.

1. Copy this folder into your skills directory:
   - user-level (available everywhere): `~/.claude/skills/soundings/`
   - or project-level: `<your-repo>/.claude/skills/soundings/`
2. Start (or restart) Claude Code.
3. Run it: `/soundings <your question>`

Then make it yours — edit `roster.md`, and start filling `self_context.md` after a few sessions.

---

## Privacy

Soundings is **local-only**. The consultation lives in your conversation; nothing it produces is aggregated or sent anywhere. Session logging is **off by default** — logs are written only when you explicitly ask, to a local location you approve. Because sessions and `self_context.md` contain first-person personal content, keep them in a gitignored location if you version-control your setup.
